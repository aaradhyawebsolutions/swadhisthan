Our Story

Swadhishthan began as more than just an idea; it was a growing vision. It emerged from the efforts of a young Onil Kulkarni, who led a group dedicated to aiding those in need during the 2013 drought. The success of their water distribution initiative, known as the Trishna project, sparked a dream in Onil's mind. This dream inspired him to pursue an education in social work, equipping him with the perspective necessary to evolve this youth group into a full-fledged NGO.

Swadhishthan’s journey, from conducting various relief drives to developing impactful awareness and sustainability programs, reflects the milestones that have shaped its growth. Today, it stands as a Non-Governmental Organization committed to driving social change by fostering greater sensitivity and resilience within communities and across government and nongovernment systems.

Our Vision

To create a noble society with empowered and responsible youth.


Our Mission

To inspire and empower youth by providing exposure, education and opportunities for social action, helping them realize their potential and become sensitive, responsible citizens who address societal issues.

Our Programs

Empowering youth through comprehensive initiatives

Gender sensitization Program 1

It is a year-long initiative designed for college students to build a strong conceptual understanding of gender-related issues.
It introduces key concepts such as gender, sex, sexuality, patriarchy, power and masculinity to help prevent gender-based violence against women.
The program is conducted in a youth-friendly manner through interactive discussions, film screenings and engaging activities.
It fosters a safe space grounded in values of non-judgment, confidentiality and respect, encouraging open dialogue and meaningful learning.

Gender sensitization Program 2

It is an advanced training for college youth who have completed Level 1 and are ready to take action through field projects.
It encourages students to create safe spaces for women and girls in their communities.
The program aims to develop a group of gender-sensitive youth leaders who can become agents of change.
These leaders work with government and non-government stakeholders to address issues related to the health and safety of women and girls.

Comprehensive Sexuality Education Program

The program is designed for students from 7th to 10th standard, focusing on their physical and mental well-being.
It helps them understand bodily and emotional changes, along with building important life and soft skills.
The program also educates students about safe and unsafe touch in an age-appropriate manner.
It includes sessions with teachers and parents to create a safe and supportive environment for adolescents.
